Methodology & Data Pipeline

This dashboard compares 1,880 job listings scraped from Bayt.com on 22 Nov 2025 with 3,544 listings scraped on 22 Feb 2026, covering Qatar's labor market. The analysis pipeline includes:

  • Data Collection: Web scraping of Bayt.com Qatar job listings with structured extraction of 19 fields per job.
  • Cleaning & Normalization: Category consolidation (e.g., "Oil and Gas" + "Oil & Gas"), education level standardization, experience range bucketing, salary parsing (monthly/yearly USD conversion), and bilingual text handling (Arabic/English).
  • NLP Skill Extraction: Semicolon-delimited skill strings parsed, lowercased, and clustered into 20 semantic groups using keyword-matching taxonomy (Leadership, SAP/ERP, AI/ML, Cybersecurity, etc.).
  • Comparative Analysis: Month-over-month delta computation across all dimensions. Job ID overlap analysis reveals Feb 2026 is a superset of Nov 2025 (all 1,880 Nov jobs persist) plus 1,664 net-new postings.
  • Supply-Demand Modeling: Sector share shifts, salary-to-demand ratios, and skill gap indicators derived from cross-tabulation of job counts, salary data, and skill frequencies.

9 Supply-Demand Analysis & Actionable Insights

▲ Sector Growth Trends

  • +233% Administrative Support is the fastest-growing category (6→20), signaling expanding back-office operations as Qatar scales its corporate infrastructure.
  • +167% Real Estate surged from 9 to 24 listings, likely driven by Lusail City development and post-World Cup property market maturation.
  • +153% Legal roles nearly tripled (15→38), reflecting growing regulatory complexity and international investment in Qatar.
  • +139% Digital & Emerging Tech skill cluster grew fastest among NLP-identified clusters, indicating Qatar's accelerating digital transformation agenda.
  • +107% Commercial Support more than doubled (111→230), suggesting growing outsourcing and business services demand.
  • Stable Construction and Oil & Gas maintain dominance (~87-89% growth), consistent with mega-project timelines (NFE, Lusail).

▲ Salary Fluctuations

  • +4.4% Overall average salary rose from $5,390 to $5,627/mo — modest but real upward pressure, driven by Healthcare and Engineering sectors.
  • +94% Engineering salaries nearly doubled ($1,833 → $3,569/mo) — strongest upward salary movement across any major sector.
  • +22% Sales sector salaries grew from $4,138 to $5,044/mo, reflecting competitive pressure for experienced sales talent.
  • −17% Hospitality salaries declined ($1,427→$1,182/mo) despite job growth — oversupply of entry-level hospitality workers is depressing wages.
  • −17% Construction salaries softened ($2,663→$2,213/mo) — high volume of lower-skilled roles diluting averages.
  • Issue Salary transparency remains critically low — only 6.3% (Nov) to 6.5% (Feb) of listings disclose pay. This is a market-wide inefficiency.

▲ Emerging Skills & NLP Findings

  • +139% Digital & Emerging Tech (IoT, automation, RPA, blockchain) — the fastest-growing skill cluster. Qatar's smart city and Industry 4.0 initiatives are driving this.
  • +98% Healthcare & Medical skills cluster nearly doubled, outpacing overall job growth. Qatar Health Strategy 2030 is creating sustained demand.
  • +98% Organizational Skills surge suggests employers are prioritizing structured, process-oriented workers as projects scale.
  • +94% Quality & Compliance skills — regulatory maturation of Qatar's market means more compliance, ISO, and audit expertise needed.
  • Core SAP/ERP remains the #1 technical differentiator — 833 mentions in Feb (84% growth). Enterprise system knowledge is required across Construction, Oil & Gas, and Services.
  • Watch AI & Data Science cluster has 1,675 mentions but many are soft-adjacent (analytical skills). Pure AI/ML roles remain niche but growing.

▼ Supply-Demand Imbalances

  • Gap Healthcare: High pay, limited supply — Highest-paying sector ($14K/mo) but only 163 jobs. The supply of qualified medical professionals is constrained; Qatar relies heavily on expatriate healthcare workers.
  • Gap Tech: 240 jobs but global competition — Tech roles are only 6.8% of market. Qatar competes with Dubai and Riyadh for tech talent, and compensation trails global benchmarks.
  • Oversupply Hospitality: Volume growth, wage decline — 429 jobs (+87%) but salaries dropped 17%. Suggests excess labor supply driving down wages for entry-level roles.
  • Mismatch Education vs. job requirements — 58% of jobs require a Bachelor's, but Construction (the biggest sector) and Hospitality (2nd biggest) predominantly need Diploma/High School. Degree inflation is real.
  • Structural Mid-level bottleneck — 213 mid-level roles vs. only 74 management roles. Career advancement funnel is narrow, risking talent flight.
  • Contract Contract work growing faster — Contracts grew 95% (98→191) vs. Full-Time at 87% (503→940). Project-based economy expanding.

★ Recommendations for Students

  • Priority Learn SAP/ERP systems — 833 job mentions; the single most requested technical skill. Certification courses have immediate ROI across Construction, O&G, and Services.
  • Priority Target 3-5 year experience band — 976 jobs require this range (the largest segment). Focus on gaining targeted, relevant experience early.
  • Path Engineering degree = maximum versatility — Opens doors to Construction (443), Engineering (274), O&G (217), Energy (52), and Consulting (26) sectors.
  • Skill Invest in project management certification — 132 jobs mention it explicitly, cutting across all sectors. PMP/PRINCE2 are high-value differentiators.
  • Language Arabic-English bilingualism commands a premium — 90 jobs explicitly require bilingual proficiency (up 73% from Nov). Bilingual candidates access 15-20% more positions.
  • Entry Hospitality and Construction for fastest entry — 872 combined jobs, many accepting Diploma/High School. Build GCC experience, then upskill into higher-paid sectors.

★ Recommendations for Policymakers

  • Urgent Mandate salary disclosure — Only 6.5% of jobs report salary. This information asymmetry harms workers and reduces market efficiency. UAE and Saudi are moving toward pay transparency regulations.
  • Strategy Invest in TVET programs — Construction (443 jobs) and Hospitality (429) are the top two sectors but largely require Diploma-level education. Technical/vocational training can rapidly fill gaps.
  • Qatarization Target mid-level roles for national workforce — 213 mid-level positions are the largest career band. Structured upskilling programs for Qatari nationals here would have maximum impact.
  • Diversify Accelerate tech sector incentives — Tech jobs are only 6.8% of market despite global digital transformation. Tax incentives, startup visas, and tech parks can diversify economy beyond hydrocarbons.
  • Healthcare Expand medical education pipelines — At $14K/mo avg salary (highest sector), investing in domestic medical training reduces expatriate dependency and retains economic value.
  • Data Establish a national labor market observatory — Regular, structured job market data collection would enable evidence-based policy. This analysis demonstrates what's possible with systematic data.

★ Recommendations for Employers

  • Advantage Post salaries to stand out — With 93.5% of competitors hiding pay, transparent listings attract significantly more qualified applicants. Early movers gain a talent acquisition edge.
  • Retention Create clearer career ladders — Only 74 management roles exist for 213 mid-level workers. The 3:1 ratio means advancement paths are limited, driving turnover.
  • Skills Build internal SAP/ERP training — The most in-demand technical skill (833 mentions) is easier to upskill internally than to hire externally at market rates.
  • Wages Hospitality: raise entry wages to reduce churn — Declining salaries (-17%) amid growing demand will increase turnover costs. Modest wage increases improve retention ROI.
  • Contracts Embrace flexible employment — Contract roles grew 95%. Offering project-based and hybrid arrangements taps a wider talent pool and reduces long-term overhead.
  • Competition Qatar Energy sets market expectations — With 330 listings (9.3% of market), their comp/benefits become the benchmark. Smaller employers must differentiate on culture, growth, or flexibility.

★ Emerging Trends to Watch

  • Rising Energy transition roles — "Energy" as a standalone category grew 68% (31→52). Qatar's LNG expansion coexists with growing sustainability/renewables hiring.
  • Rising Digital infrastructure buildout — "Senior Digital Roll-Out Engineer" tripled (2→6). Large-scale digitalization projects are underway across utilities and government.
  • Rising Legal & compliance maturation — Legal roles grew 153%, the highest rate of any professional sector. Qatar's maturing regulatory environment demands more legal expertise.
  • Rising Aerospace cluster forming — 48 aerospace jobs (Strategic Aerospace Services) in Feb. Qatar's defense and aviation maintenance sector is crystallizing.
  • Watch Contract economy expanding — Contract roles growing faster than permanent (+95% vs +87%). This structural shift has implications for labor law, benefits, and worker protection.
  • Watch AI/ML still nascent but accelerating — While "AI & Data Science" skill cluster shows 84% growth, most mentions are analytical skills rather than pure AI roles. The transition is underway but early-stage.
